# Project Charter Framework

The **Project Charter** framework is designed to define the foundational elements of a project, ensuring clarity on its scope, objectives, and stakeholders. It serves as a guiding document to align teams and stakeholders on the intent and goals of the project.

## Purpose
The Project Charter framework helps teams:
- Define the scope, objectives, and stakeholders of a project.
- Establish a clear understanding of the problem space and intended solutions.
- Outline the measures of success and expected outcomes.

## Sections
The Project Charter includes the following sections:

1. **Name of the Project**: A clear and concise title for the project.
2. **Start Date**: The date the project begins.
3. **End Date**: The expected completion date of the project.
4. **Project Background**: Context and history leading to the initiation of the project.
5. **Intent**: The purpose or motivation behind the project.
6. **Goal**: The overarching aim or objective of the project.
7. **Definition of Problem Space**: A detailed description of the problem the project seeks to address.
8. **Solution Description**: A high-level overview of the proposed solution.
9. **In-Scope Infrastructures/Products/Services/Mechanisms**: A list of what is included within the project’s scope.
10. **Expected Outcomes**: The results or deliverables anticipated from the project.
11. **Measures of Success**: Criteria or metrics to evaluate the project’s success.
12. **Key Stakeholders**: Individuals or groups with a vested interest in the project.
13. **Potential Partnerships**: Organizations or entities that could collaborate on the project.
14. **Location**: The geographical area or context where the project will be implemented.

## Interaction Methods
Users can interact with the Project Charter framework in three ways:
1. **Upload Files**: Upload documents, images, or other files to auto-fill sections of the framework.
2. **Chat with AI**: Answer questions from the AI agent to generate a completed framework.
3. **Guided Steps**: Follow detailed instructions to manually complete each section.

## Output
The completed Project Charter will be presented as a structured document that can be:
- Downloaded as a PDF.
- Saved to the user’s profile for future reference or reuse.
