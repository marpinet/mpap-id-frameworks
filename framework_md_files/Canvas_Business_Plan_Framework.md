# Canvas Business Plan Framework

The **Canvas Business Plan** framework provides a high-level overview of a business model, helping users visualize and structure their business ideas. It is based on the traditional Business Model Canvas structure.

## Purpose
The Canvas Business Plan framework helps teams:
- Define and communicate the key components of their business model.
- Identify value propositions, customer segments, and revenue streams.
- Align stakeholders on the business strategy.

## Sections
The Canvas Business Plan includes the following sections:

1. **Key Partners**: Identify the external organizations, suppliers, or partners that are critical to the success of the business.
   - Example: A coffee shop partnering with local bakeries to offer fresh pastries.
2. **Key Activities**: Outline the most important tasks and actions the business must perform to operate successfully.
   - Example: A software company focusing on continuous product development and customer support.
3. **Key Resources**: List the assets (physical, intellectual, human, or financial) required to deliver the value proposition.
   - Example: A fashion brand relying on its design team and supply chain network.
4. **Value Propositions**: Define the unique value the business delivers to its customers.
   - Example: A meal delivery service offering healthy, pre-portioned ingredients for quick home cooking.
5. **Customer Relationships**: Describe how the business interacts with and retains its customers.
   - Example: A subscription box service providing personalized customer support and loyalty rewards.
6. **Channels**: Identify the methods and platforms used to deliver the value proposition to customers.
   - Example: An e-commerce store using its website and social media for sales and marketing.
7. **Customer Segments**: Define the target audience or groups of customers the business serves.
   - Example: A fitness app targeting young professionals seeking convenient workout solutions.
8. **Cost Structure**: Summarize the major costs associated with operating the business.
   - Example: A manufacturing company with high costs in raw materials and labor.
9. **Revenue Streams**: Outline how the business generates income from its value propositions.
   - Example: A streaming platform earning revenue through subscriptions and advertisements.

## Definitions
- **Value Proposition**: The unique value a company offers to its customers, solving a problem or fulfilling a need.
- **Customer Segments**: Specific groups of people or organizations a business aims to serve.
- **Revenue Streams**: The various ways a business earns money from its customers.

## Interaction Methods
Users can interact with the Canvas Business Plan framework in three ways:
1. **Upload Files**: Upload documents or business plans to auto-fill sections of the framework.
2. **Chat with AI**: Answer questions from the AI agent to generate a completed framework.
3. **Guided Steps**: Follow detailed instructions to manually complete each section.
