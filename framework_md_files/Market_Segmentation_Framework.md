# Market Segmentation Framework

The **Market Segmentation** framework helps users divide their target market into distinct groups based on shared characteristics and identify the most valuable segments to prioritize. It provides a structured approach to understanding customer needs and tailoring strategies for each segment.

## Purpose
The Market Segmentation framework helps teams:
- Divide the target market into distinct groups based on shared characteristics.
- Identify the most valuable segments to prioritize for a product or service.
- Develop tailored strategies to meet the needs of each segment.

## Sections
The framework includes the following components:

1. **Market Overview**: Provide a high-level description of the overall market.
   - Example: The global fitness industry, valued at $100 billion.
2. **Segmentation Criteria**: Define the criteria used to segment the market (e.g., demographics, psychographics, behavior, geography).
   - Example: Segmenting by age group, income level, and fitness goals.
3. **Segment Profiles**: Create detailed profiles for each segment, including:
   - Characteristics: Key traits or attributes of the segment.
     - Example: Young professionals aged 25-35 with disposable income.
   - Needs: Specific needs or pain points of the segment.
     - Example: Convenient workout solutions for busy schedules.
   - Preferences: Buying behaviors, preferences, or habits.
     - Example: Preference for app-based fitness programs.
4. **Segment Value Assessment**: Evaluate each segment based on:
   - Market Size: The potential size of the segment.
     - Example: 10 million potential customers in the target age group.
   - Profitability: The revenue potential of the segment.
     - Example: High spending on premium fitness services.
   - Accessibility: The ease of reaching and serving the segment.
     - Example: High accessibility through digital marketing.
   - Strategic Fit: Alignment with the organization’s goals and capabilities.
     - Example: Strong alignment with the company’s focus on digital solutions.
5. **Prioritization**: Rank segments based on their value and strategic importance.
   - Example: Prioritizing young professionals due to their high profitability and alignment with the company’s goals.

## Definitions
- **Market Segmentation**: The process of dividing a target market into distinct groups based on shared characteristics.
- **Segment Profiles**: Detailed descriptions of each market segment, including characteristics, needs, and preferences.
- **Strategic Fit**: The alignment between a market segment and the organization’s goals and capabilities.

## Interaction Methods
Users can interact with the Market Segmentation framework in three ways:
1. **Upload Files**: Upload market research reports or customer data to auto-fill sections of the framework.
2. **Chat with AI**: Answer questions from the AI agent to generate a completed segmentation framework.
3. **Guided Steps**: Follow detailed instructions to manually complete each section.

## Output
The completed Market Segmentation framework will be presented as:
- A structured document summarizing market segments, their value, and the action plan.
- A downloadable PDF version of the framework.
- An option to save the framework to the user’s profile for future reference or reuse.
